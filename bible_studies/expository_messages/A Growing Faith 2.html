Introduction:

In This Life We Stumble

  • The Obvious Stumble of The Unsaved The Bible teaches us that without Christ we are absent of light. In fact we are so deprived of light that our own heart deceives us into believing we can see SENSORY DEPRIVATION Jer 17:9  The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it? 
    • The "Feel-Good" Lie This is why the world is constantly looking for "So-Called" Truths that feel good and tickle the ear 2Ti 4:3  For the time will come when they will not endure sound doctrine; but after their own lusts shall they heap to themselves teachers, having itching ears;  2Ti 4:4  And they shall turn away their ears from the truth, and shall be turned unto fables. 
    • We have to be careful When Coming to church, be open to what you need to hear, not what you want. There is only one truth that is right, that is His truth (Joh 14:6  Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me. )
  • The less Obvious Stumble of the Saved We want to believe that when we are saved, all of our problems go away but this is contrary to the Word of God. In fact its likely that you will face even more adversity than before. This adversity is known as Spiritual Warfare.
    • Stumbling Doesnt Come from Adversity but By Getting Your Eyes of Christ How Can You See if You Dont Have The Light of God illuminating YOUR PATH Psa 119:105  NUN. Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path. 
  • The Magnitude of The Stumble ("fall")
    • The Danger of The Soft Stumble Truth is when we fall it should be our prayer that we fall hard and have no worldly success. Without the HARD FALL, we dont see OUR NEED For Christ. This is what Jesus was teaching us when He said its easier for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle.... Mat 19:23  Then said Jesus unto his disciples, Verily I say unto you, That a rich man shall hardly enter into the kingdom of heaven. ||**|| Mat 19:26  But Jesus beheld them, and said unto them, With men this is impossible; but with God all things are possible. 
    • The Severity of The Hard Fall Ive seen a lot of people sober up but had no foundation they were building their life on. What ends up happening is these people, not being rooted, go back to where they came from and end up worse then what the started. Turn To Matt 12:43-45 "Mat 12:43  When the unclean spirit is gone out of a man, he walketh through dry places, seeking rest, and findeth none.  Mat 12:44  Then he saith, I will return into my house from whence I came out; and when he is come, he findeth it empty, swept, and garnished.  Mat 12:45  Then goeth he, and taketh with himself seven other spirits more wicked than himself, and they enter in and dwell there: and the last state of that man is worse than the first. Even so shall it be also unto this wicked generation. "

    • You Must Hit Bottom Notice in our text, "He raiseth up the poor out of the dust" There is no lower place than the dust
    • He Lifts You Up And Sets You Among Princes God's intention is not to lift you up and leave you where you are. Yes God meets you where you're at but God wants to work in you, and to set you among royalty

  • The Growth of Faith Also Has One Path it comes from obedience and followship to Jesus Christ

    Before Peter's Victory Over the Supernatural, He First Had to Be Solid on The Fundamentals of Christianity

    • Obedience To Christ Was First Sometimes we put the cart before the horse. We know God is real, and we know He has a supernatural will for our lives but we want to get straight into our calling without having a solid foundation. The first step of every believer has to be obedience and followship of Jesus Christ Mat 14:22  And straightway JESUS CONSTRAINED his disciples to get into a ship, and to go before him unto the other side, while he sent the multitudes away.
    • Peter Was a Member of The Church The Church is the training ground of every Christian Mat 14:22  And straightway Jesus constrained HIS DISCIPLES to get into a ship, and to go before him unto the other side, while he sent the multitudes away. 
      • There is a difference between a believer and a Christian Act 11:26  And when he had found him, he brought him unto Antioch. And it came to pass, that a whole year they assembled themselves with the church, and taught much people. And the DISCIPLES WERE CALLED CHRISTIANS first in Antioch 
    • Peter Believed God Was Capable To often we turn to worldly solutions to our problems before God because we think our problems are too big for God Mat 14:28  And Peter answered him and said, Lord, if it be thou, bid me come unto thee on the water.
      • If You Need Help With Housing - Ask God
      • You Want Your Family To Trust You Again - Ask God
      • You Want Peace in Your Life Beyond Understanding, Ask God
      • SEEK HIM FIRST Mat 6:33  But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you.

    After The Fundamentals Are In Place We Still Have To Get Off The Boat

    • 1) You Have To Listen To The Invitation If youre hear this morning then God is Persuing you and has put a Call on Your Life. Will You listen to His Invitation Mat 14:29  And he said, COME.
    • 2) Take A Step Towards God Hearing God's voice above the noise of this world is an amazing thing, but if were not careful we will leave out the critical next step of action Mat 14:29 And when Peter was come down out of the ship,
    • 3) Get Off The Boat It's easy to walk towards God when you still in familiar territory. The true act of Faith comes when you let go of the known, and step into the unknown where you only have God to guide you. Mat 14:29 "...And when Peter was come down out of the ship, he walked on water,..."
      • Its okay to step out slowly illustration of getting of the boat - taking your time and testing the water What is keeping you on the boat. The boat as a representation of the world.
      • Prove God, step on the water, but hold onto the boar If youre not there yet thats okay, but prove God. Follow His requirement and let Him fulfill His promise to you. The Bible is filled with promises allowing you to grow your faith. But it does take courage.
      • Eventually you are going to have to let go of the boat and step towards God to see the true miracle of being wholly dependent upon Him Matt 14:29 "...he walked on the water, to go to Jesus."

    Staying Above Water

    • Keep Your Eyes Upon Jesus At first Peter makes no mention of the storm, but somewhere along the line he stopped looking at God and started focusing on the world around him Mat 14:30  But when he saw the wind boisterous, he was afraid;
    • Get Your Eyes On Jesus Before Its Too Late The Sad part of this story is not that Peter noticed the storm, but that he fixated on it so much that he let it take him out. Are you going through a storm right now that is taking your attention more then Jesus. Sad Reality seen in Mat 14:30 "...HE WAS AFRAID" He no longer felt the comfort and peace of the Lord in His life
    • Remember Your Saviour Peter's saving grace here was that he remembered the one who can save him when all hope seemed lost. If you get in a position where it seems helpless, cry out to God and see if He is not faithful to rescue you. Matt 14:30 "...he cried, saying, Lord, save me.  Mat 14:31  And immediately Jesus stretched forth his hand, and caught him, and said unto him, O thou of little faith, wherefore didst thou doubt? "
      • Dont let mistakes Keep You From God The Bible Says in "1Jn 1:9  If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness. "

    God's Response To Our Falls

      Notice that God is never caught off Guard. Nothing You are going through surprises Him or causes Him to not want to intervene.

      • His Intervention Is Immediate Mat 14:31  And IMMEDIATELY Jesus stretched forth his hand,
      • He Gives Us a Fathers Instruction Matt 14:31 "...and said unto him, O thou of little faith, wherefore didst thou doubt? "
        • Notice the reprimand, but not the forsaking Heb 13:5  Let your conversation be without covetousness; and be content with such things as ye have: for he hath said, I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee. 
      • He Quiets The Storms Mat 14:32  And when they were come into the ship, the wind ceased.
