What is Baptism?

  • Definition The term "baptism" in English is derived from the Greek word "baptisma" (βάπτισμα). This term originates from the Greek verb "baptizō" (βαπτίζω), which means "to immerse" or "to submerge."
    • Note that it is submerged, not sprinkled on
  • Baptism is Christ's Death Symbolized He, in amazing grace, because of our sins, bore on the Cross the awful baptism of divine wrath, that we might enjoy forever the living favor of the GOD of all grace Romans 6:3 "Know ye not, that so many of us as were baptized into Jesus Christ were baptized into his death?"
  • Baptism Is A Picture Of Our Death With Him m. If He died beneath the judgment of GOD for sin, it was as taking our place Romans 6:4 "buried with him by baptism into death; that like as Christ was raised up from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life"
  • Baptism Is Our Christian Uniform A man being recruited for the army, the moment he signs the articles is a soldier. But the uniform marks him out as such. Galatians 3:27 "For as many of you as have been baptized into Christ, have put on Christ"

Can I Be A Christian Without Baptism?

  • Baptism Is Commanded It is our chance to show we are all in, our first directive from God Act 2:38  Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost. 
  • It is Required To Be A Member Of The New Testament Church According to the Scriptures baptized believers (the church) are the body of CHRIST: 1Co 12:13  For by one Spirit are we all baptized into one body, whether we be Jews or Gentiles, whether we be bond or free; and have been all made to drink into one Spirit.
