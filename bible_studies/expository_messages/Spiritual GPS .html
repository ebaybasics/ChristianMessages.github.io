Galatians 5:16 (KJV): "This I say then, Walk in the Spirit, and ye shall not fulfil the lust of the flesh."

Spiritual GPS Series Intro

  • Overview of Galatians:
    • Penned by Paul around 48 AD
    • Christianity was exploding in numbers 25 years after the death of Christ and Christianity was growing outside of Judea and Jerusalem, all through the Roman Empire
    • Gentiles for the first time were being grafted into the vine Not by works but through Faith in Christ alone

The Core Message of the Book of Galatians

  • Salvation Through Faith Alone False teachers were spreading lies.Many Jews were angry that Gentiles were given access to Salvation, even more so that it was told they could forgo the circumcision. They were adding to salvation Ephesians 2:8 declares, "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God."
  • Salvation is Evidenced by The Fruit Of The Spirit A dead tree does not produce divine fruit. But once we are born again we are given Spiritual life. Our Spiritual fruit is evidence of this life
  • Sanctification is Evidenced by the Fruit of the Spirit People that are overwhelmed by the cares of this world are going to produce less fruit A tree that is choked out by thorns and thistles will not produce fruit. This happens when we are walking in the course the world lays out, not God

The Aim of This Series

  • 1) To Explore The Divine Guidance of God
  • 2) To Understand How To be in Tune with This Guidance

    The Necessity for Divine Guidance: The War Within (Galatians 5:16-18)

      Gal 5:16  This I say then, Walk in the Spirit, and ye shall not fulfil the lust of the flesh.  Gal 5:17  For the flesh lusteth against the Spirit, and the Spirit against the flesh: and these are contrary the one to the other: so that ye cannot do the things that ye would.  Gal 5:18  But if ye be led of the Spirit, ye are not under the law. 

      • The Nature Of The Battle Gal 5:16  This I say then, Walk in the Spirit, and ye shall not fulfil the lust of the flesh. 
        • The battle is not external but internal, between the Spirit and the flesh. For the flesh lusteth against the Spirit, and the Spirit against the flesh:
        • This is not a mere struggle; it's a war with eternal consequences. I didnt witness to Sara because I didnt want to seem like a Bible thumper
      • The Impossibility of Neutrality Illustration: Trying to be neutral in the spiritual battle is like trying to stand in the middle of a busy highway. You'll get hit. Neutrality is a deception; it's actually a vote for the flesh.
      • The Promise of Victory Walk in the Spirit, and YOU WILL NOT fulfill the lust of the flesh.

      The Fruit of the Spirit: The Inevitable Outcome of a Spirit-Led Life

        Gal 5:22  But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith,  Gal 5:23  Meekness, temperance: against such there is no law. 

        • The Nature of the Fruit A tree doesn't strive to produce fruit; it's the natural outcome of being a healthy tree. Similarly, the fruit of the Spirit is the natural outcome of a life rooted in God. Gal 5:22 But the fruit of the Spirit
        • The Unity of the Fruit Notice it's "fruit," not "fruits"—it's a unified package that comes from the Spirit.
        • The Counterfeit of the Flesh The flesh always tries to counterfeit these fruits. This is where Works for Salvation gets mistaken for Works caused by Salvation
          • Illustration: Kids wearing winter clothes in Summer time

        The Holy Spirit: The Divine Convictor and Guide

          Gal 5:19  Now the works of the flesh are manifest, which are these; Adultery, fornication, uncleanness, lasciviousness,  Gal 5:20  Idolatry, witchcraft, hatred, variance, emulations, wrath, strife, seditions, heresies,  Gal 5:21  Envyings, murders, drunkenness, revellings, and such like: of the which I tell you before, as I have also told you in time past, that they which do such things shall not inherit the kingdom of God.

          • The Role of the Holy Spirit We understand these things are wrong because of the Holy Spirit The Holy Spirit convicts us of sin and guides us into righteousness.
            • He is not a mere influence; He is the third Person of the Trinity.
          • The Works of the Flesh Works are produced by us, the fruit is produced by the Spirit that dwells in us
            • These works are destructive and lead to death. Even people that live contrary to God understand that the works of the flesh lead to death Illustration: Worldly parent telling child to do as I say not as I do
          • The Necessity of Sensitivity
            • Being sensitive to the Spirit's leading is crucial. After years of listening to our impulses to make decisions it is not something that happens overnight but we must train ourselves to be sensitive to the speaking of the Holy Spirit. This is sanctification
            • Conviction is God's Mercy Warning When we dont listen to the conviction its analgous to taking an iron to our conscience 1Tim 4:2 "Speaking lies in hypocrisy; having their conscience seared with a hot iron;"

          Prayer: The Sole Means of Divine Communion

            Gal 4:6  And because ye are sons, God hath sent forth the Spirit of his Son into your hearts, crying, Abba, Father. 

            • The Nature of Prayer Prayer is not a ritual, but a conversation between you and Jehovah Gal 4: 6 And because ye are sons,
            • The Spirit's Role in Prayer The Spirit helps us in our weakness, even in our prayers. Rom 8:26 "Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered."
            • The Necessity of Persistent Prayer Prayer should be a daily, non-negotiable discipline. We brush our teeth everyday because it will keep our teeth from falling out. How much more important is it to speak with our Heavenly Father

            Closing

            • The Battle Within Listen to me very carefully. You are not a spectator; The Spirit and the flesh are at war within you, and you can't be neutral. Neutrality is a lie that sends you straight to the enemy's camp.
            • The Unmistakable Mark of a True Believer: The Fruit of the Spirit Don't tell me you're a Christian if your life doesn't bear the fruit of the Spirit. These aren't mere human attributes; these are divine qualities that manifest in a life genuinely surrendered to Christ.
            • The Indispensable Necessity of Prayer and Sensitivity to the Spirit Prayer isn't a religious exercise; it's the very breath of the Christian life. And the Holy Spirit isn't some impersonal force; He's the third Person of the Trinity, convicting you of sin and guiding you into righteousness. If you're not sensitive to the Spirit's leading, you're walking in darkness, no matter how religious you appear to be.
