Notice Our Scripture (Context): Php 4:6 Be careful for nothing; BUT IN EVERY THING by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known unto God.

  • An Unlikely Author:
    • Written By Paul Paul was born and raised Jewish, and eventually would become a Pharisee (A very religous sect that hated the Christian Movement)
    • He Was a Christian Bounty Hunter He is known for his involvement in the murder of Christians and chasing them down
    • His transformation to a Christian would have left him alone I want you to understand the significance of Paul's conversion, and the high worldly costs associated with it
  • He Wrote This Letter to the Persecuted Church of Philippi
    • Encouraging them not to be afraid of their enemies: Php 1:28 And in nothing terrified by your adversaries: which is to them an evident token of perdition, but to you of salvation, and that of God.Â 
    • Reminding them To Be Christians in The Good Times and Bad Php 1:27 Only let your conversation be as it becometh the gospel of Christ: that whether I come and see you, or else be absent, I may hear of your affairs, that ye STAND FAST in one spirit, with one mind striving together for the faith of the gospel;
  • Paul was no Stranger to Persecution 2Co 11:24 Of the Jews (Remember Paul Was a Jewish Pharisee) five times received I forty stripes save one. 2Co 11:25 Thrice was I beaten with rods, once was I stoned, thrice I suffered shipwreck, a night and a day I have been in the deep; 2Co 11:26 In journeyings often, in perils of waters, in perils of robbers, in perils by mine own countrymen, in perils by the heathen, in perils in the city, in perils in the wilderness, in perils in the sea, in perils among false brethren; 2Co 11:27 In weariness and painfulness, in watchings often, in hunger and thirst, in fastings often, in cold and nakedness. 2Co 11:28 Beside those things that are without, that which cometh upon me daily, the care of all the churches.

The Call To Be Anxious for Nothing:

    Php 4:6 Be careful for nothing;

    • We Live in a World of Instant Gratification
      • If I want to know something I search Google: And if I have to wait more than a few seconds I get irritated. We want answers at the snap of our fingers.
    • We Expect God To Give Us Things Instantly I was talking to a man a while back and was telling him about our housing program. He didnt like that he had to wait 4 Sundays before he qualified for our house, He wanted in right away. The sad truth is, that same man months later is still without a place to sleep.
    • An Invitation and Opportunity To Trust God Are you going through something right now and hoping God will give you an answer. Being careful for nothing is both an invitation to trust God, but an opportunity to show God you trust Him

    The Combination of a Successful Prayer Life: Php 4:6 "but in every thing by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known unto God"

    • Illustration: Remembering my Locker in Highschool The great struggle. If you get a number wrong you have to start over and try again. I remember struggling often trying to figure this out. In this same way Paul is teaching us in what manner to pray...Prayer + Supplication + Thanksgiving
    • 1) Approach God With Humility The very idea of Prayer is that you understand That He is God and has power to answer your request,
    • 2) Surrender Your Problem To Him Another way of thinking of Supplication is pleading with God to answer your request. It's a picture of Surrendering it over to Him, and its the instruction Paul gives us
    • 3) Do so with Thanksgiving Remember all that He has done, and will do
    • 4) Trust His Answer Sometimes God closes doors that we were trying to go through. It is important to trust Him, because if we focus on the closed door we will miss the one He has opened for us
      • Illustration: Child support letter
