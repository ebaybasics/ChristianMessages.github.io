The Popular True Story of Jonah

  • The Greatest Fish Story Of All Time As we follow Jonah's journey, we take a front row to seeing the struggle of serving God versus serving self
  • A Story of Rebelion God calls Jonah to the mission field, but Jonah turns his back on God and goes in the other direction
  • A Story of God's Grace and Mercy No matter how far we run, God's mercy can reach us, and now matter how great our transgressions, His grace can redeem us.
    • The fish of Judgement God is a God of consequence, and righteous judgement. Being in the whale was uncomfortable Psalm 96:13: "Before the LORD: for he cometh, for he cometh to judge the earth: he shall judge the world with righteousness, and the people with his truth."
    • The fish of refuge Even in God's Judgement we see His Mercy, protecting Jonah from the sea Psalm 46:1: "God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble."
  • A Story of God Using Imperfect Vessels I'm glad that God can still use imperfect people like myself
  • A Story of Humility Jonah's pride brought him low, but his humility allowed Him to be lifted up Proverbs 22:4: "By humility and the fear of the LORD are riches, and honour, and life."
  • A Story of a Nations Revival 2 Chronicles 7:14: "If my people, which are called by my name, shall humble themselves, and pray, and seek my face, and turn from their wicked ways; then will I hear from heaven, and will forgive their sin, and will heal their land."

Jonah is Called To Nineveh

    Jon 1:2  Arise, go to Nineveh, that great city, and cry against it; for their wickedness is come up before me. 

    • The Time It Was Written The text places Jonah in about 700bc, so a couple of generations after David and a couple of generations before the time of Daniel.
    • Nineveh was a great metropolis of Assyria It had a population of more than 120,000 people Jon 4:11  And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than sixscore thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and also much cattle? 
    • Historical records suggest the walls of Nineveh spread 7.5 miles
    • Assyrians were Pagan and worshipped believed in many different Gods
    • Assyrians were natural enemies of the Jewish people In fact, between 800-700bc the assyrian people would conquer the Jewish armies 2Ki 17:6  In the ninth year of Hoshea the king of Assyria took Samaria, and carried Israel away into Assyria, and placed them in Halah and in Habor by the river of Gozan, and in the cities of the Medes.
