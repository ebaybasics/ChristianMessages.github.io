The Foundation Series Week 2: 1 Cor 3:11 For other foundation can no man lay than that is laid, which is Jesus Christ. 

Iron Shackles:

Rom 6:14  For sin shall not have dominion over you: for ye are not under the law, but under grace. 

    From Shackles to Ruin

    • The deception of sin (When we first pursue pleasure, it is pleasurable for a season) Heb 11:25  Choosing rather to suffer affliction with the people of God, than to enjoy the pleasures of sin for a season; 
    • Sin struggles mixed with deception (After a while it becomes a lifestyle, some days tired of it) Pro 4:19  The way of the wicked is as darkness: they know not at what they stumble. 
    • Devastation of the shackles (Been shackled so long, you dont even notice >> Given up) Rom 1:24  Wherefore God also gave them up to uncleanness through the lusts of their own hearts, to dishonour their own bodies between themselves: 

      The Characteristics of the Shackles

    • Origin of Sin (Adam's willful decision to reject God's authority led to our imprisonment) Rom 5:12  Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned: 
    • The Shackles Bring Darkness (Theres a reason why we ask "why is everything we try not working") Joh 3:19  And this is the condemnation, that light is come into the world, and men loved darkness rather than light, because their deeds were evil. 
    • Separated from God (When shackled to sin, we are enemies of God. The one that we call to help we separate from) Isa 59:2  But your iniquities have separated between you and your God, and your sins have hid his face from you, that he will not hear. 

      The Realization of Being Shackled - Hitting the Bottom

    • No Longer Under the Illusion of Sin (You start realizing how much sin has taken from you) Isa 42:16  And I will bring the blind by a way that they knew not; I will lead them in paths that they have not known: I will make darkness light before them, and crooked things straight. These things will I do unto them, and not forsake them. 
    • Everything seems more difficult (Sin has made even the most simple tasks (getting an id) difficult) Mat 11:28  Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest. 
    • Constantly Tired, Ready for Change (This is where God wants us to be so He can intervene) Mat 11:28  Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest. 
      • Who is burned out with the way things are going?

      Benefits of Being Unshackled

    • A New Vision (No more stumbling in darkness) Psa 119:105  NUN. Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path. 
    • Some New Friends (Brother Mike and I are going to help you get on your feet) Gal 6:2  Bear ye one another's burdens, and so fulfil the law of Christ. 
      • Would it make a difference if you had someone on your team helping you get housing?
    • A New Life Optional: Elaborate the Topic 2Co 5:17  Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new. 

      Removing the Shackles

    • Seek Him: Mat 6:33  But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you. 
    • Remember who you are: Rom 5:8  But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us. 
    • Repent: Mat 4:17  From that time Jesus began to preach, and to say, Repent: for the kingdom of heaven is at hand. 
    • Surrender to Him: Rom 10:9  That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved. 

      Closing

    • Are you tired of your situation
    • Will you walk away today rejecting God's intervention on your life
    • Are you ready for a new life today? -Come, let us take a Bible and show you what God has for you, and what you must do to receive His gift
