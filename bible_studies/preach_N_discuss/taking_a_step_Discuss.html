Approaching God:

James 4:8-10 "Draw nigh to God, and he will draw nigh to you. Cleanse your hands, ye sinners; and purify your hearts, ye double minded. Be afflicted, and mourn, and weep: let your laughter be turned to mourning, and your joy to heaviness. Humble yourselves in the sight of the Lord, and he shall lift you up."

    Introduction

    • Question your motives for being here. Did you come to use the house of God for your benefit? (I did too) God knows: Isa 55:8 "For my thoughts are not your thoughts, neither are your ways my ways, saith the LORD."
    • Maybe you don't know why you came: Php 2:13 "For it is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of his good pleasure."
    • If you came because you wanted to hear from God, you took a step toward Him. God is not a liar, He draws closer to you. What are you going to do about it? Psa 145:18 "The LORD is nigh unto all them that call upon him, to all that call upon him in truth."

    The Promise for Approaching God:

    • You will be lifted up:
      • Jas 4:10 "Humble yourselves in the sight of the Lord, and he shall lift you up."
        • What do you think James means when he says to "Humble yourselves"? Why is that important?
          • Possible Answers: Surrender, Hit a bottom
      • Mat 6:33 "But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you."
    • God is on your team: Rom 8:31 "What shall we then say to these things? If God be for us, who can be against us?"
    • You will never again be forsaken: Deuteronomy 31:6 "Be strong and of a good courage, fear not, nor be afraid of them: for the Lord thy God, he it is that doth go with thee; he will not fail thee, nor forsake thee."
      • Has anybody ever written you off, or gave up on you? -Personal testimony of how people wrote me off

    How to Approach God: and Make it Last

    • You must be saved.
      • If God loves us, What's keeping us separated from Him?
        • Answer: Our sin is what seperates us from God
          • Rom 6:16 "Know ye not, that to whom ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye obey; whether of sin unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness?"
            • The Bible calls this separation death - we are dead to God by our own choosing, God doesn't want this: Rom 6:23 "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord."
    • Turn from your way to His:
      • Act 3:19 "Repent ye therefore, and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out, when the times of refreshing shall come from the presence of the Lord;"
        • What do you think the Bible means when it tells us to repent? Does that mean we need to be perfect or better before God will save us?
          • Answer:God simply means that we need to have a broken heart about our decisions, to understand that our way is killing us and His way leads to life
    • The answer is through Jesus Christ, will you accept it? Rom 10:13 "For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."

    A New Walk

      1Sa 2:8 "He raiseth up the poor out of the dust, and lifteth up the beggar from the dunghill, to set them among princes, and to make them inherit the throne of glory: for the pillars of the earth are the LORD'S, and he hath set the world upon them."

    • When you accept Christ, you accept an everlasting promise: Joh 10:28 "And I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand."
    • It's time to start putting away old things, once you accept God's salvation for you you are a new creation: 2Corinthians 5:17 "Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new."
    • You are a part of a royal family: You are a part of the family of God. As a part of this family, you need to be active in His house: Heb 10:25 "Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together, as the manner of some is; but exhorting one another: and so much the more, as ye see the day approaching."
    • You are not alone: But you are only as good as your training. You must get involved in your training: Pro 27:17 "Iron sharpeneth iron; so a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend."
